About

The Applied Econometrics Reading Group is a graduate-level initiative to delve into key econometric papers, explore cutting-edge methods, and foster discussions. Meetings are held every two weeks, focusing on a mix of theoretical and applied topics.

Significant efforts have been made to integrate Machine Learning and Deep Learning methods into the fields of economics and econometrics. Surveys on the economics applications of Machine Learning and Deep Learning:

  • Mullainathan and Spiess (2017)
  • Athey and Imbens (2019)
  • Dell (2024)
  • Zheng, Xu and Xiao (2022)

Protocol

  1. Each graduate student participant is required to present at least once.
  2. Regular meetings will be held every two weeks.
  3. Two presentations per meeting, each consisting of a 30-minute presentation followed by a 15-minute Q&A session.
